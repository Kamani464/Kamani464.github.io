Meet the R46

The R46 subway car is a gritty and enduring fixture of New York City's transit scene. Since hitting the tracks in the 1970s, these tough silver cars with their faux wooden interiors have been hauling passengers across the city. You'll find them rumbling along the A, C, N, Q, R, and W lines, surviving the daily grind with a mix of resilience and character. Despite their aging bones and occasional quirks, they remain a lifeline for commuters, braving the city's underground labyrinth day in and day out. Fun Facts

  • The R46 subway cars were built by the Pullman Standard Company between 1975 and 1978.
  • They are recognizable by their stainless steel exteriors and faux wooden interiors.
  • R46 cars primarily operate on the A, C, N, Q, and W lines in the New York City subway system.
  • These cars were initially designed for the IND Second System
  • The R46s have a seating capacity of 44 passengers per car and can accommodate hundreds more standing.
  • Each R46 car is approximately 75 feet long and weighs around 85,000 pounds.
  • These subway cars have undergone several refurbishments over the years to extend their lifespan and improve passenger comfort.
